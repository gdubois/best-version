The Qwen 3.5 4B model at 4-bit quantization (Q4_K_M) is currently the best balance of quality and speed for a Raspberry Pi 5 with 8GB RAM, delivering 4–5 tokens per second with significantly better results than other 4-bit variants. 

While smaller models like Qwen 3.5 2B offer faster speeds (5–6 t/s) and Qwen 3.5 0.8B can reach 8.05 t/s, the 4B model is widely recommended for 8GB devices as it provides superior performance without exceeding memory constraints, whereas the 9B variant is generally not suitable for this hardware. 

Recommended Model: qwen3.5:4b (4-bit quantized, specifically Q4_K_M). 
Performance: ~4–5 tokens/second on standard 8GB Pi 5. 
Memory Usage: Fits comfortably within 8GB, leaving headroom for the OS.
Setup: Use Ollama or llama.cpp with optimized settings (e.g., OLLAMA_MAX_LOADED_MODELS=1) to ensure stability. 
Alternative: For maximum speed, Qwen 3.5 2B is a strong runner-up, while Phi-3.5 Mini or Gemma 2 2B remain viable options for specific use cases.
